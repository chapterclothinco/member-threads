## 1F. FAQ Section

### Comprehensive FAQ Answers

**Q: What's the minimum order size?**
"Our minimum is typically 24 pieces per design, though this can vary based on the product type and customization complexity. We know that's higher than print-on-demand sites, but there's a reason: we're actually manufacturing your apparel, not just printing on blanks. If you're working with a smaller group, reach out anyway — we can sometimes combine similar orders or suggest alternatives that work for smaller quantities."

**Q: What do you mean by 'truly custom'?**
"When most companies say 'custom,' they mean they'll print your logo on a pre-made shirt from their catalog. We're different. We manufacture apparel from scratch — selecting fabrics, creating patterns, and constructing each piece specifically for your order. This means you're not limited to catalog colors, standard fits, or off-the-shelf styles. Want your exact brand color in a specific fabric weight with a tailored fit? We can do that. This is true custom manufacturing, not decorated blanks."

**Q: How long does it take?**
"Typical turnaround is 4-6 weeks from design approval to delivery, though this varies based on complexity and quantity. Rush options are available for time-sensitive projects — just let us know your deadline upfront and we'll tell you what's possible. We always recommend reaching out at least 8 weeks before your event date to give plenty of buffer for the design process and any committee approvals you need."

**Q: We don't have a designer. Can you help?**
"Absolutely — most of our clients don't have design resources. Our in-house team will create custom concepts based on your input. All you need to provide is your logo (any format), your brand colors (or colors you like), and some direction on the vibe you're going for. We'll handle the rest. You'll typically receive 2-3 initial concepts, and we include revision rounds to refine until your committee is happy. This design support is included in our service — it's not an extra charge."

**Q: Do you work with nonprofits and their budgets?**
"We work with nonprofit organizations every day and understand the budget realities. While we're not the cheapest option (because we use premium materials and true custom manufacturing), we offer several ways to work within nonprofit budgets: simpler designs that reduce production costs, material options at different price points, quantity breaks for larger orders, and phased ordering for organizations that want to start small. We'll always be transparent about pricing and help you find the best value for your budget."

**Q: Can you ship directly to our event venue?**
"Yes! We can ship to event venues, conference centers, camp facilities, or anywhere else your gear needs to end up. We can also coordinate multi-location delivery for organizations with chapters or distributed teams. Just let us know your logistics needs and we'll build it into the plan. We include tracking information so you always know where your order is."

You'll pay more than the cheapest online options, but you're getting manufactured apparel — not printed blanks. ## 2B. Customization Options

### Custom Fabrics

**What it is:**
"We can manufacture your apparel in custom fabrics — including exact color matches to your brand standards, specialty blends (organic cotton, recycled materials, performance fabrics), and custom weights. This goes far beyond picking from a catalog."

**When to choose this:**
Organizations with strict brand standards who need exact color matches, groups prioritizing sustainability who want specific eco-friendly materials, or anyone who wants something truly unique that can't be found off the shelf.

**Considerations:**
Custom fabrics typically add lead time (2-3 weeks) and work best with larger orders (75+ pieces) to be cost-effective. We'll discuss options during the design phase.

---

### Screen Printing

**What it is:**
"Traditional screen printing creates vibrant, durable graphics by pressing ink through a mesh screen. It's ideal for bold designs with solid colors and delivers excellent longevity — your graphics will outlast the garment itself."

**When to choose this:**
Best for designs with limited colors (1-4), bold graphics, and larger print areas. Most cost-effective for larger quantities since screen setup costs are spread across more pieces.

**Considerations:**
Each color requires a separate screen, so complex multi-color designs or photographic images may be better suited for other methods. We'll recommend the best approach during design review.

---

### Embroidery

**What it is:**
"Embroidery stitches your design directly into the fabric, creating a textured, premium look that never fades or cracks. It's the gold standard for a polished, professional appearance."

**When to choose this:**
Perfect for logos on polos, jackets, and hats. Ideal when you want a premium, corporate-quality look. Works beautifully for smaller designs and text-based logos.

**Considerations:**
Very detailed designs or photographic images don't translate well to embroidery. Large embroidered areas can add stiffness to lighter fabrics. Best for designs under 5" wide for most applications.

---

### Custom Patches

**What it is:**
"Custom patches — woven, embroidered, or leather — add a tactile, premium element to any apparel. They can be permanently attached during manufacturing or designed as removable accessories members can place on their own gear."

**When to choose this:**
Great for a vintage, heritage aesthetic. Perfect for organizations that want members to have flexibility in where/how they display affiliation. Excellent for military-style or outdoor/adventure organizations.

**Considerations:**
Patches add a small per-unit cost but create a distinctive, premium look. Removable patches work well as standalone merchandise items.

---

### Custom Labels

**What it is:**
"Replace the standard manufacturer tag with your organization's own woven or printed label. This subtle detail creates a fully branded experience and eliminates the 'blank with a logo' feel entirely."

**When to choose this:**
Any organization that wants a polished, fully-custom feel. Especially valuable for merchandise you're selling, premium member gifts, or any situation where you want the apparel to feel intentional rather than decorated.

**Considerations:**
Custom labels add minimal cost and we recommend them for almost every project. We can include care instructions on custom labels to maintain a clean, tag-free interior.

---

### Custom Packaging

"Elevate the unboxing experience with custom packaging — including branded boxes, tissue paper, hang tags, stickers, and inserts. Turn receiving your apparel into a moment worth sharing."

## 10B. Case Study Template

### Case Study Structure

**1. Header Section**
- Client name and logo (with permission)
- Organization type
- Project type
- Key metric/outcome (e.g., "200 custom pieces for annual fundraiser")
- Hero image of final products

**2. The Organization (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Who they are and what they do
- Their community and members
- Why apparel matters to their mission

**3. The Challenge (2-3 paragraphs)**
- What prompted this project
- Previous apparel experiences (pain points)
- Specific requirements or constraints
- Timeline and budget considerations

**4. The Solution (3-4 paragraphs)**
- How Member Threads approached the project
- Design process and decisions
- Product selection and customization choices
- Any unique elements or creative solutions

**5. The Process (brief timeline)**
- Key milestones from first contact to delivery
- How long each phase took
- Any adjustments along the way

**6. The Results (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Final products delivered
- Client reaction and feedback
- Member/community response
- Any measurable outcomes (pieces distributed, events outfitted, etc.)

**7. Client Quote (pull quote)**
- Compelling testimonial from key contact
- Should summarize the experience and outcome

**8. Gallery**
- 4-8 images showing:
  - Products (flat lay and on people)
  - Products in use at events
  - Detail shots
  - Before/after (if applicable)

**9. Project Specs (sidebar or bottom)**
- Products created (with quantities)
- Customization methods used
- Timeline
- Notable features

### Information to Gather from Clients

1. Permission to feature (written)
2. Organization background and mission
3. Why they started this project
4. Previous apparel experiences
5. Their specific requirements
6. How they found Member Threads
7. Their experience with the process
8. Feedback from their members
9. High-resolution photos (products, events, team)
10. A quotable testimonial

### Suggested Length
- Full case studies: 800-1,200 words
- Mini case studies (for portfolio): 200-300 words
- Format: Web page with generous imagery, also available as PDF download
